Welcome to Victoria's Restaurant

Victoria's Restaurant began as a stubborn little idea sketched on the back of a napkin by Victoria Mulugeta after a rainy day farmers' market when she swapped stories (and recipes) with an old chef who taught her the perfect roast. She spent a summer restoring an abandoned carriage house, planting herbs in the courtyard, and inviting neighbors over for Sunday suppers that quickly turned into long lines and warm, loud dinners. The menu blends her abuela's slow-cooked stews with bright, market-driven salads and a rotating pastry that became the town's obsession.
